CPF Retirement Account · CPF LIFE · Interest Mechanics

What happens to your CPF RA interest from age 55, 65 and beyond 70?

A self-contained guide to the questions discussed: whether RA interest is “returned”, when CPF LIFE annuity premium is deducted, and whether interest continues after payouts start.

Prepared as of 23 June 2026 For members turning 55 in 2026 Uses Basic / Full / Enhanced RA levels

Executive summary

The key point is simple: the interest is not usually paid to you as a separate cash item. It is accumulated in your RA before payouts start, and then factored into CPF LIFE payouts after the annuity premium is deducted.

1. Age 55: RA is created

Your Retirement Account is created at 55. Savings from your Special Account, then Ordinary Account, are transferred to your RA up to the Full Retirement Sum.

2. Age 65–70: payout decision window

You may start CPF LIFE payouts from 65, or defer up to 70. The CPF LIFE premium is deducted one week before payouts start.

3. After payouts start: interest continues

Your CPF LIFE premium continues to earn CPF interest. The interest is factored into monthly payouts and supports lifelong payouts.

Plain-English answer: Your 4% RA interest from 55 to 65 is not lost, but it is also not normally paid out as a separate cheque at 65. It becomes part of the retirement savings that support your CPF LIFE monthly payout.

Timeline: what happens at each age?

55

RA is created

Your RA is opened. SA savings, then OA savings, are transferred to your RA up to the FRS. RA savings earn CPF interest and are meant to provide future payouts.

55–65

Interest accumulates

The RA continues to earn interest. The accumulated interest becomes part of the RA balance that supports future monthly payouts.

65–70

Payout start window

You can start payouts from 65 or defer up to 70. If you defer CPF LIFE payouts, they can increase by up to 7% for each year deferred.

70+

Payouts must start

If not already started, payouts start automatically at 70. CPF LIFE premium continues earning interest, which is factored into monthly payouts.

Questions and answers

Q1. After my CPF RA is created at age 55, will I get back the 4% interest accumulated from age 55 to 65?

Yes, you benefit from it, but not usually as a separate interest payout. The interest is added into your RA balance over time. When you start retirement payouts, the larger RA balance supports your CPF LIFE monthly payout.

Q2. Does CPF buy the CPF LIFE annuity at age 55 or age 65?

Not at age 55. Age 55 is when the RA is created. CPF LIFE premium is deducted from your RA one week before you start payouts, which can be any time from age 65 to 70.

Q3. If I choose the CPF LIFE Standard or Escalating Plan, what happens to my RA?

For the Standard and Escalating Plans, the savings in your RA are deducted as the CPF LIFE annuity premium when payouts start. Your monthly payouts are then paid for as long as you live.

Q4. What if I choose the CPF LIFE Basic Plan?

Under the Basic Plan, only about 10% to 20% of RA savings is deducted as the CPF LIFE premium when payouts start. The remaining 80% to 90% remains in the RA and is used for monthly payouts until one month before you reach age 90. The Basic Plan generally gives lower monthly payouts than the Standard Plan.

Q5. Is interest still earned after age 65?

Yes. If you defer payouts from 65 to 70, your retirement savings continue to support a higher future payout. CPF states that CPF LIFE payouts increase by up to 7% for each year of deferral.

Q6. Is interest still earned after age 70?

Yes, but payouts cannot be deferred beyond 70. If payouts have not started by then, they start automatically. CPF LIFE premium continues to earn CPF interest, and the interest is factored into monthly payouts rather than paid separately.

Q7. What happens if my CPF LIFE premium is depleted while I am still alive?

CPF LIFE is designed to provide payouts for life. CPF explains that monthly payouts are first paid from your CPF LIFE premium. When that premium is depleted, payouts continue from accumulated pooled interest, no matter how long you live.

Q8. Does the interest on CPF LIFE premium go to my beneficiaries when I pass away?

CPF states that if you pass away before your CPF LIFE premium is exhausted, any remaining premium balance and remaining CPF savings are distributed to your beneficiaries. However, the interest on CPF LIFE premiums supports the lifelong payout pool and is not treated as a separate bequest item.

Practical interpretation for retirement planning

What you should focus on

  • RA amount at age 55: BRS, FRS or ERS level determines the base from which future payouts are estimated.
  • Payout start age: Starting at 65 gives earlier income; deferring up to 70 can increase monthly payouts.
  • CPF LIFE plan choice: Standard gives steady payouts, Escalating grows 2% yearly, Basic starts lower and leaves more in RA initially.
  • Separate cash vs lifelong income: RA interest is best understood as supporting monthly retirement income, not as a separate fixed deposit-style interest payout.

One-line conclusion

CPF does not “take away” the interest. The interest is embedded into the RA / CPF LIFE mechanics and used to support higher and lifelong monthly payouts.
